# Responsible AI at Work Lab

Practical open tools for small and mid-sized organizations adopting AI in real business workflows.

## Project

The first project is the **Responsible AI Adoption Toolkit for SMEs**: a lightweight set of templates, guides, examples, and risk-management materials that help organizations pilot AI responsibly while measuring productivity, workforce impact, job quality, human oversight, and governance risk.

This project is intentionally practical. It is for operators who need to answer:

- Should we use AI in this workflow?
- Who is affected?
- What productivity baseline exists?
- What could go wrong?
- Who remains accountable?
- What human review is required?
- When should a pilot stop, revise, expand, or scale?

## Current status

**Draft v0.2** — early public draft.

The toolkit is usable as a starting point, but it is not legal advice, compliance certification, or a substitute for qualified review in high-impact settings.

## Why this exists

AI adoption is accelerating inside ordinary organizations. Large enterprises can hire governance teams; smaller organizations usually cannot. This creates a gap between high-level responsible-AI frameworks and day-to-day workflow decisions.

Responsible AI at Work Lab translates responsible-AI principles into practical artifacts that small organizations can use before, during, and after AI pilots.
