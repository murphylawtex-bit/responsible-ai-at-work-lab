Responsible AI at Work Lab

Practical open tools helping small and mid-sized organizations adopt AI responsibly while measuring productivity, workforce impact, human oversight, and governance risk management.

View the toolkit on GitHub Quickstart NIST AI RMF Crosswalk

Why this exists

AI adoption is entering ordinary work faster than many organizations can govern it. Large enterprises can hire AI governance teams; small and mid-sized organizations usually cannot. This project translates responsible-AI principles into workflow-level tools operators can actually use.

The Lab starts with a designated human lead: a Responsible AI Subject Matter Expert, or small SME team, inside the participating organization. That person or team coordinates AI integration, connects the tools to operational reality, and keeps governance grounded in human-led oversight, clear policies, escalation paths, and risk management.

What the toolkit helps answer

Should we use AI here?

Define the workflow, AI role, users, affected people, and intended outcome.

What value is real?

Measure baseline productivity, quality, rework, review burden, and user experience.

Who is affected?

Map workforce impact, job quality, autonomy, transparency, fairness, and stakeholder feedback.

Who remains accountable?

Define human oversight, review rules, override paths, escalation, and stop conditions.

Hardware & technology considerations

Responsible AI adoption depends on more than policies and prompts. Teams also need to assess whether the actual work environment can support the intended AI use case safely, reliably, and securely.

Infrastructure fit

Review devices, network access, cloud or local deployment needs, and whether existing systems can support the workflow.

Security and access

Define identity, permissions, data storage, retention, endpoint controls, and vendor/security constraints before launch.

Integration readiness

Identify required software integrations, data sources, user support needs, maintenance responsibilities, and fallback procedures.
