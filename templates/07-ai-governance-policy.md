# Lightweight Responsible AI Governance Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how the organization pilots and adopts AI tools responsibly, with attention to business value, human accountability, workforce impact, privacy, fairness, and public trust.

## Scope

Applies to AI tools used for drafting, summarization, analysis, recommendations, automation, customer interaction, operational decision support, or employee-facing workflows.

## Principles

1. **Human accountability** — A person remains accountable for AI-assisted decisions.
2. **Purpose limitation** — AI is used only for approved purposes.
3. **Transparency** — Affected users/workers should know when AI materially shapes a workflow.
4. **Data minimization** — Use only the data needed; avoid sensitive/confidential inputs unless approved.
5. **Fairness and access** — Assess whether outcomes differ unfairly across groups.
6. **Worker participation** — Affected workers should provide feedback before scale-up.
7. **Measurement** — Productivity claims must be measured against a baseline.
8. **Reversibility** — Pilots should be limited and stoppable before full scale.

## Required review before pilot

- AI Use Case Intake completed
- Stakeholder and Impact Map completed
- Risk Register created
- Human Oversight Plan approved
- Measurement Plan approved

## Prohibited uses without special review

- Sole basis for hiring, firing, discipline, pay, promotion, scheduling, lending, housing, insurance, legal, medical, safety, or other high-impact decisions
- Covert employee surveillance
- Uploading confidential, personal, regulated, or customer data into unapproved tools
- External communications without human review
- Automated impersonation of a person without disclosure and approval

## Pilot requirements

- Defined owner
- Defined duration
- Baseline metrics
- Worker/user feedback channel
- Incident log
- Review meeting before expansion

## Review cadence

- Pilot review: end of pilot or 30 days, whichever comes first
- Production review: quarterly or after major model/vendor/workflow changes

## Incident response

Material errors, data exposure, unfair outcomes, or scope violations must be logged, escalated to the owner, and reviewed before the workflow continues.
