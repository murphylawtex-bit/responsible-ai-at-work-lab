Quickstart

A practical seven-step path for reviewing an AI pilot before it becomes business-as-usual.

HomeExamplesNIST CrosswalkGitHub
Rule of thumb: If nobody can explain who is affected, what value is measured, and who remains accountable, the AI pilot is not ready to scale.

The 7-step review

1

Define the use case

What workflow changes? What AI does what? Who owns it?

Use-case intake template

2

Identify affected people

Who uses it, who is affected, and could it change job quality, access, fairness, privacy, or trust?

Stakeholder map · Workforce checklist

3

Measure value

What is the baseline? What improvement is expected? How will quality and rework be measured?

ROI measurement plan

4

Register risk

What can go wrong? Who owns the risk? What controls are required?

Risk register

5

Keep humans accountable

What decisions stay human-owned? Who reviews AI output? Who can override it?

Human oversight plan

6

Pilot narrowly

Use limited scope, limited data, clear stop conditions, and a real feedback channel.

30-day implementation guide

7

Review before scale

Stop, revise, continue, expand, or scale only after evidence review.

Pilot scorecard · Decision memo

Decision signals

Green

Clear use case, low data sensitivity, measurable baseline, human owner, worker feedback, reversible pilot.

Yellow

Useful idea but unclear metrics, incomplete stakeholder review, or weak mitigation ownership.

Red

High-impact decisions, sensitive data, unclear accountability, no feedback path, or hard-to-reverse rollout.
