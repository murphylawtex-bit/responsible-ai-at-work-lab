# Worker and Stakeholder Feedback Guide

## Purpose

AI adoption changes work. This guide helps organizations collect practical feedback from people affected by an AI pilot before deciding whether to scale.

## When to use

Use this guide:

1. Before a pilot starts.
2. Mid-pilot if the workflow changes materially.
3. At pilot review before scale-up.

## Principles

- Feedback should be safe to give honestly.
- Workers should know what AI is being tested and why.
- Feedback should influence the pilot decision.
- Productivity metrics should not be the only evidence considered.

## Pre-pilot questions

1. Which parts of this workflow are most frustrating or repetitive today?
2. Where do errors, delays, or rework usually happen?
3. What would make AI assistance useful here?
4. What would make AI assistance harmful or burdensome?
5. What information should not be put into an AI system?
6. Which decisions should remain human-owned?
7. What would you need to trust this workflow?

## Mid-pilot questions

1. Is the AI saving time, shifting work, or creating extra review burden?
2. Are outputs accurate enough to be useful?
3. Are people over-trusting the AI?
4. Are people ignoring the AI because it is not useful?
5. Has the pilot changed autonomy, workload, stress, or skill use?
6. Have any privacy, fairness, or transparency concerns appeared?
7. What should be changed before continuing?

## End-of-pilot questions

1. Should this workflow stop, revise, expand, or scale?
2. What evidence supports that decision?
3. What risks remain unresolved?
4. What training would be needed before wider use?
5. What should managers not do with this AI output?
6. What would make the workflow more transparent or fair?

## Minimum standard before scale-up

Do not scale if:

- affected people were not informed;
- feedback was not collected;
- high-impact concerns remain unresolved;
- review burden exceeds productivity gain;
- human accountability is unclear.
