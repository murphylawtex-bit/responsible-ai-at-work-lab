# 30-Day Responsible AI Adoption Pilot Guide

## Objective

Run a limited, measurable AI adoption pilot that creates real business evidence while protecting workers, customers, and public trust.

## Week 1 — Define and screen

1. Pick one narrow workflow.
2. Complete `01-ai-use-case-intake.md`.
3. Complete `02-stakeholder-and-impact-map.md`.
4. Create initial `05-risk-register.md`.
5. Decide whether the use case is safe enough for a limited pilot.

Deliverable: pilot charter and risk screen.

## Week 2 — Measure and govern

1. Complete `03-productivity-roi-measurement-plan.md`.
2. Complete `06-human-oversight-plan.md`.
3. Adopt or adapt `07-ai-governance-policy.md`.
4. Brief affected workers/users.

Deliverable: approved pilot plan.

## Weeks 3–4 — Pilot

1. Run the AI workflow in parallel or limited production.
2. Track time, quality, rework, review burden, and incidents.
3. Collect worker/user feedback.
4. Update the risk register weekly.

Deliverable: pilot evidence file.

## End of Month — Review

1. Complete `08-pilot-review-scorecard.md`.
2. Decide: stop, revise, expand, or scale.
3. Document conditions required for next stage.

Deliverable: adoption decision memo.

## Good pilot characteristics

- Narrow enough to reverse.
- Clear human owner.
- Baseline exists.
- No high-impact decisions without special review.
- Workers/users can give feedback.
- Risks are tracked visibly.

## Bad pilot characteristics

- Vague productivity claims.
- No baseline.
- No human accountability.
- Sensitive data in unapproved tools.
- Hidden worker surveillance.
- “Human in the loop” that is really rubber-stamping.
