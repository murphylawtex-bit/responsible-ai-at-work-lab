# Example: Sales Proposal Drafting Pilot

## Scenario

A small B2B company wants to use AI to prepare first-draft proposal outlines from notes, prior templates, and customer requirements.

## AI role

The AI drafts structure and language. Humans own pricing, commitments, technical accuracy, legal terms, and final sending.

## Baseline

- Average proposal drafting time: 4–6 hours
- Rework from missing requirements: frequent but not tracked
- Proposal quality varies by salesperson

## Pilot design

- Duration: 6 proposal cycles
- Scope: internal draft generation only
- Human review: 100% before external use
- Data rule: no confidential customer data in unapproved tools

## Metrics

| Metric | Baseline | Target |
|---|---:|---:|
| Draft preparation time | 4–6 hr | 2–3 hr |
| Missing-requirement rework | untracked | track and reduce |
| Reviewer satisfaction | unknown | 4/5 average |
| Customer-facing errors | 0 tolerance | 0 |

## Key risks

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Hallucinated product/technical claims | Source-linked requirement checklist |
| Wrong commercial terms | Pricing/legal fields excluded from AI ownership |
| Confidentiality breach | Data minimization and approved tools |
| Deskilling | Use as assistant; train staff on review quality |

## Worker feedback focus

- Does AI improve draft speed without lowering quality?
- Are drafts easy to review or subtly misleading?
- Does the tool help junior staff learn proposal structure?

## Decision rule

Expand only if draft time improves and human reviewers confirm quality, accuracy, and review burden are acceptable.
