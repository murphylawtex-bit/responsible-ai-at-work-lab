# Responsible AI at Work Lab — Initiative Brief

## One-sentence thesis

Responsible AI at Work Lab helps small and mid-sized organizations adopt AI in real business workflows while measuring productivity, workforce impact, job quality, human judgment, and governance risk.

## Problem

AI adoption is moving faster than most small and mid-sized organizations can responsibly manage. Large enterprises can hire AI governance teams, but SMEs often adopt tools informally: a few employees experiment, managers hear promises of productivity, and workflows change before anyone has measured risks, worker impact, or actual ROI.

That gap matters. AI can improve productivity and expand capability, but careless adoption can also create hidden surveillance, deskilling, unfair outcomes, privacy leaks, overreliance on hallucinated outputs, and brittle workflows. The organizations most exposed to these risks often have the fewest practical tools for managing them.

## Proposed solution

Build and distribute an open, practical Responsible AI Adoption Toolkit for SMEs. The toolkit translates responsible-AI principles into lightweight templates, workflows, and eventually software-assisted assessment tools that operators can use before, during, and after AI pilots.

The first version includes AI use-case intake, stakeholder and societal impact map, productivity / ROI measurement plan, workforce impact checklist, AI risk register, human oversight plan, responsible AI governance policy, pilot review scorecard, 30-day implementation guide, and example SME pilot.

## Why this matters now

Most AI adoption guidance is either too abstract for operators or too enterprise-heavy for small organizations. SMEs need practical tools that answer: should we use AI here, who is affected, how do we measure benefit, what could go wrong, and what human controls are required?

This project aims to make responsible AI adoption usable at the business-process level, not just the policy level.

## Initial audience

- Small and mid-sized business operators
- Workforce and economic-development organizations
- Civic-tech and public-interest technology groups
- Researchers studying AI adoption and job quality
- Open-source responsible AI practitioners

## Early deliverables

1. Public-ready toolkit v0.1
2. NIST AI RMF crosswalk
3. 3–5 example use cases
4. Lightweight scoring method for use-case readiness and risk
5. Pilot measurement framework
6. Partner/interview list for future field validation

## Funding fit

The project fits individual grants, open-source grants, public-interest technology grants, AI governance grants, future-of-work funding, and non-dilutive startup/R&D funding if later expanded into a software product.
