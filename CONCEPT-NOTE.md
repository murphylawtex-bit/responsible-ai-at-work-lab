# One-Page Concept Note

## Project title

Responsible AI at Work Lab: Practical tools for responsible AI adoption in small and mid-sized organizations

## Summary

Responsible AI at Work Lab will create open, practical tools that help small and mid-sized organizations adopt AI responsibly in real business workflows. The project focuses on the point where AI leaves theory and enters daily work: drafting, summarization, analysis, workflow automation, customer communication, decision support, and employee productivity.

The goal is to help organizations measure value while protecting human judgment, job quality, privacy, transparency, and public trust.

## Problem

AI adoption is accelerating inside SMEs, but most organizations lack a simple way to evaluate use cases before they scale. Existing AI governance frameworks are often too abstract, legalistic, or enterprise-heavy for smaller teams. As a result, AI pilots can proceed without baseline measurements, worker feedback, risk ownership, or clear human accountability.

This creates two failures at once: organizations may overestimate productivity gains, and they may underestimate workforce and societal risks.

## Solution

The project will build and improve an open Responsible AI Adoption Toolkit for SMEs. The toolkit will include templates, guides, scorecards, and eventually lightweight software-assisted workflows for AI use-case intake, productivity and ROI measurement, workforce and stakeholder impact review, job-quality and worker-participation checks, human oversight and accountability, AI risk register and governance controls, and pilot review and scale/stop decisions.

## Initial artifact

Version 0.1 already exists as a working internal draft with templates for use-case intake, stakeholder impact, ROI measurement, workforce impact, risk registration, human oversight, governance policy, pilot review, implementation guidance, and an example SME pilot.

## Expected outcomes

- SMEs gain practical tools for evaluating AI pilots responsibly.
- Funders/researchers gain a framework for studying AI adoption effects in real workflows.
- Workers and affected stakeholders gain more visibility and voice in AI-enabled changes.
- The public-interest AI ecosystem gains reusable open-source materials.

## Funding request concept

Initial funding would support toolkit refinement, open-source publication, user interviews, example pilots, documentation, and early outreach to SME/workforce partners.

## Why now

AI adoption is already happening informally. The question is not whether organizations will use AI; it is whether they will adopt it with measurement, judgment, and responsibility.
